  • Exercise 2: A light bulb (2.4 V and 0.7 A) is sphere-shaped with a diameter of 1 cm. Find the radiant flux, radiant intensity, radiant exitance, and energy emitted in 5 minutes.
    • The radiant flux (power) is calculated as:
      Φ = IV = 2.4 V × 0.7 A = 1.68 W
    • Radiant intensity is given by:
      I = Φ / Ω
      where Ω is the solid angle of a sphere (4π sr). Thus:
      I ≈ 0.1337 W/sr
    • Radiant exitance (M) is:
      M = Φ / A
      with the surface area of the sphere being A = 4πr². Given the radius r = 0.5 cm, the radiant exitance is:
      M ≈ 1337 W/m²
    • The energy emitted in 5 minutes is:
      E = P × t = 1.68 W × 300 s = 504 J
    Radiant flux: 1.68 W.
    Radiant intensity: 0.1337 W/sr.
    Radiant exitance: 1337 W/m².
    Energy emitted in 5 minutes: 504 J.
