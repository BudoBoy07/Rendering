  • Exercise 1: A small 25 W light bulb has an efficiency of 20%. How many photons are approximately emitted per second? Assume in the calculations that we only use average photons of wavelength 500 nm.
    • To calculate the number of photons emitted by the light bulb, we must first determine the energy of a single photon. The energy of a photon is given by the equation:
      Ephoton = hc / λ
      where h is Planck's constant, c is the speed of light, and λ is the wavelength. Substituting the given wavelength (500 nm), we obtain an approximate energy per photon of:
      Ephoton ≈ 4 × 10-19 J
    • Next, we compute the number of photons emitted per second using:
      n = (P × η) / Ephoton
      where P is the power of the light bulb and η is its efficiency. Given that the power is 25 W and the efficiency is 20%, the number of photons emitted per second is approximately:
      n ≈ 1.25 × 1019 photons/s
    Answer: Approximately 1.25 × 1019 photons are emitted per second.
